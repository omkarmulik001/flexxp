How to do the Incline Bench Press – Step-by-Step Guide

  • Step 1: Sit on an incline bench. Make sure the bench is set to an incline of 15-30 degrees.
  • Step 2: Wrap your fingers throughout the bar with your palms facing apart from you.
  • Step 3: As you inhale, slowly lower yourself until the bar is an inch off her chest. Arms should be at your sides, at a 45-degree angle.
  • Step 4: Hold this position for every count to the end of this movement, exhale and return the bar to the starting position.
  • Step 5: Fold your arms and go down slowly.

Incline Bench Press Overview

The flat bench adaptation is upper body bread and butter for powerlifters, but the incline press is an absolutely useful exercise. The incline bench press implants even more shoulder work to help with log presses and other overhead events. Bodybuilders uses incline presses into their programs to work the entire chest. Regardless of your lifter type, you may benefit from including this variation of the bench press in your routine.

Benefits of The Incline Bench Press

The Incline Barbell Bench Press is a great way to work your upper chest.

The Incline Bench Press works your chest, lats, triceps, and shoulder muscles.

The Incline Barbell Bench Press helps build muscle, increase strength and endurance, and boost your metabolism.

The Incline Barbell Bench Press is one of the best ways to strengthen your triceps because it puts more stress on them.
