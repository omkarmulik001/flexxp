Frog Jumps Form

  • Start in a squat position with hands touching the ground.
  • Explode upward, extending your body and reaching for the sky.
  • Land softly back into the squat position.
  • Ensure your knees are aligned with your toes during each jump.
  • Engage your core and use your arms for balance.
  • Repeat the explosive jumps for a complete set.

Jumping Split Lunges

  • Start in a lunge position, one foot forward and one foot back.
  • Lower into a lunge, ensuring both knees are bent at a 90-degree angle.
  • Explosively jump upward, switching your leg positions mid-air.
  • Land softly back into the lunge position. Keep your back straight, chest up, and engage your core.
  • Repeat the dynamic jumps for a complete set.

Pause Jump Squat

  • Begin in a standard squat position, feet shoulder-width apart. Lower into a squat, keeping your back straight.
  • Pause briefly at the bottom with thighs parallel to the ground. Explosively jump upward, extending your hips and knees fully.
  • Land softly, returning to the squat position.
  • Maintain a controlled rhythm, pausing at the bottom before each explosive jump. Repeat for a complete set, focusing on precision.

Paused Squats

  • Start with feet shoulder-width apart, toes slightly turned out. Lower into a squat, keeping your back straight and chest up.
  • Hold the paused position for a brief count (1-3 seconds).
  • Push through your heels to stand back up.
  • Repeat for a complete set, focusing on controlled pauses.

Reverse Bodyweight Lunge

  • Stand with feet hip-width apart, hands on your hips or extended in front.
  • Step one foot backward, lowering your body into a lunge position. Ensure both knees are bent at a 90-degree angle.
  • Push through the front heel to return to the starting position.
  • Repeat on the other leg, alternating lunges. Keep your core engaged for balance and stability.

Single Leg Squat

  • Stand on one leg, keeping the other foot lifted. Extend your arms for balance.
  • Lower your body by bending the standing knee. Aim for a 90-degree bend in the knee
  • Keep your core engaged and chest up. Push through the heel to return to the starting position.
  • Repeat on the other leg, alternating single leg squats.

Barbell Back Squat

  • Set a barbell on a squat rack at about chest height. Load it with an appropriate weight.
  • Stand facing the bar, feet shoulder-width apart. Duck under the bar, resting it across your upper back, not on your neck.
  • Grip the bar with hands slightly wider than shoulder-width. Unrack the bar and take a step or two back.
  • Engage your core, keep your chest up, and lower your body by bending your knees and hips simultaneously.
  • Go as low as your flexibility allows or until your thighs are parallel to the ground. Push through your heels to stand back up, extending your hips and knees.
  • Push through your heels to stand back up, extending your hips and knees. Repeat the movement, maintaining proper form.

Dumbbell Goblet Squat

  • Stand with your feet shoulder-width apart, holding a dumbbell vertically with both hands close to your chest.
  • Keep your chest up, shoulders back, and core engaged throughout the movement.
  • Lower your body into a squat position by bending your knees and pushing your hips back, as if sitting into a chair.
  • Lower yourself until your thighs are parallel to the ground, ensuring your knees track in line with your toes.
  • Push through your heels to return to the starting position, fully extending your hips and knees.

Dumbbell Squat

  • Stand with feet shoulder-width apart, holding a dumbbell in each hand at your sides.
  • Engage your core, keep your chest up, and lower your body by bending your knees and pushing your hips back.
  • Lower yourself until your thighs are parallel to the ground or as far as your flexibility allows.
  • Ensure your knees track over your toes and don't cave inward.
  • Push through your heels to stand back up, fully extending your hips and knees.

Dumbbell Step Up

  • Stand in front of a sturdy bench or step with a dumbbell in each hand, arms by your sides.
  • Place your right foot on the bench, ensuring the entire foot is secure.
  • Push through your right heel, lifting your body upward until your right leg is fully extended.
  • Bring your left foot up, placing it beside your right foot on the bench.
  • Step back down with your right foot, followed by the left.
  • Repeat the movement, alternating leading legs

Dumbbell Sumo Squat

  • Stand with feet wider than shoulder-width, toes pointing outward.
  • Hold a dumbbell in each hand, arms extended down. Lower into a deep squat, keeping your back straight.
  • Ensure knees align with toes, thighs parallel to the ground. Push through heels to stand, engaging inner thighs and glutes.
  • Repeat for a complete set.

Dumbbell Leg Extension

  • Sit on a bench with a dumbbell in each hand, legs together.
  • Secure the dumbbells between your feet. Extend your legs fully, lifting the dumbbells toward the ceiling.
  • Ensure controlled movements and a straight leg extension. Lower the dumbbells back down, maintaining a steady pace
  • Keep your back against the bench and core engaged throughout. Perform leg extensions for a complete set.

Machine Hack Squat

  • Adjust the machine to fit your body, ensuring the shoulder pads are comfortably positioned on your shoulders and your feet are placed shoulder-width apart on the platform.
  • Grasp the handles on the sides of the machine for stability. Keep your back against the backrest and maintain a neutral spine throughout the exercise.
  • Lower your body by bending your knees, allowing the platform to descend.
  • Lower yourself until your thighs are parallel to the ground or slightly below, ensuring your knees track in line with your toes.
  • Push through your heels to extend your knees and return to the starting position.

Kettlebell Goblet Squat

  • Stand with your feet slightly wider than shoulder-width apart, holding a kettlebell close to your chest with both hands, elbows pointing down.
  • Engage your core, keep your chest up, and maintain a neutral spine.
  • Lower your body by bending your knees and pushing your hips back, as if sitting into a chair.
  • Lower yourself until your thighs are parallel to the ground, ensuring your knees track in line with your toes.
  • Push through your heels to return to the starting position, fully extending your hips and knees.
